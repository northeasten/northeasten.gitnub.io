IN ITALY

Italy is the land of the espresso, where it is drunk strong and fast, standing at the counter. At about 11 a.m., which is colazione time (a short midmorning break halfway between breakfast and lunch), people commonly treat themselves to an espresso accompanied by a little pastry. At home, Italian caffèmoka reigns supreme. The Italians do not drink filter coffee.

IN SCANDINAVIA (NORWAY, SWEDEN, ETC.)

In nineteenth-century Norway it was common for people to produce their own spirits at home. Before long, to combat alcohol consumption, the Church decided topromote a less dangerous drink: coffee. And when home distillation was banned, coffee consumption became the norm, and has remained so ever since.
